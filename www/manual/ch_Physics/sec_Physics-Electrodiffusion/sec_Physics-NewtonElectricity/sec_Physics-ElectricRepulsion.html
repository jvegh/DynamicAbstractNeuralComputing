Ions repulsion

We assume that two N⁢a+ ions are at a distance of 1⁢n⁢m. The force between them is

FN⁢a+=14*π*ϵo⁢q1*q2r2=9*109⁢(1.60217663*10-1910-9)2=2.311027*10-12⁢N (2.50)

If we assume that a 100,000⁢V/m gradient[35] exists across the membrane, the value of the force is

FN⁢a+=105*1.60217663*10-19=1.6*10-12⁢N (2.51)

Given that the mass of a N⁢a+ ion is mN⁢a=3.8175458*10-26⁢k⁢g, it causes an acceleration

a=FN⁢a+mN⁢a+=6.0517*1015⁢ms2 (2.52)

The distance d to travel is the thickness of the neuronal membrane 5*10-9⁢n⁢m and we assume a continuosly accelerating ion, and using d=12*a*t2

tt⁢r⁢a⁢v⁢e⁢r⁢s⁢e=2*5*10-96.05*1015=0.165*10-12⁢s (2.53)

In this period the ion is accelerated to v=a*t the speed

vN⁢a+=1.65*10-13*6.0517*1015=103⁢m/s (2.54)

That is, with the assumption that the N⁢a+ ions are at a distance of 1⁢n⁢m, the ions starting at the beginning of the ion channel will speed up to 103⁢m/s and will pass the ion channel in 0.165⁢p⁢s.

At the same time, a K+ ion is mK=6.4732*10-26⁢k⁢g, it causes an acceleration

a=FN⁢a+mN⁢a+=3.569*1015⁢ms2 (2.55)

With this acceleration, the K+ ions traverse d=12*3.569*1015*(0.165*10-12)2=0.294*10-9⁢n⁢m
