Connecting to Fick’s Law

By expressing the speed through the Stokes-Einstein relation, see Eq.(1.11)

d⁢Vd⁢t=D*R*R*Tq*F*F*d2⁢Cd⁢x2=(T*R2q*F2)*D*d2⁢Cd⁢x2 (1.23)

Given that Fick’s Second Law of Diffusion is expressed as

d⁢Cd⁢t=D*d2⁢Cd⁢x2 (1.24)

we can derive the ratio between the electric and thermodynamic temporal gradients.

d⁢Vd⁢t=2.3*10-6*D*d2⁢Cd⁢x2=2.3*𝟏𝟎-𝟔⁢d⁢Cd⁢t (1.25)

We can provide a rough experimental estimation. The time course of gradient(half width) in Fig. 4.10 was produced in 0.3*103⁢s. Reciprocally, a voltage gradient needs 0.1*10-3⁢s to produce the same concentration gradient, see Figs. 4.7 and 4.4. Their ratio is 3*10-6.

The other way round, when we assume that the propagation of the electric field in the solution is 2*108⁢m/s and multiply it by the factor above, we arrive at the speed of ion’s speed in the range around 102⁢m/s.
