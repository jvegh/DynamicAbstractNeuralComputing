§ 1.2.1 Creating the brain

Let us play with the idea that we are assisting God (or Evolution) in creating the World, and our task is to design the brain. The materials and Laws of the World (including the non-living and living science) are already fixed and we need to produce something for controling biological objects, from the already available objects and the rest of the budget.

We must consider several constraints. The brain we design, just to mention a few major items, must

  • •

    be governed by the available Laws

  • •

    be inexpensive (both its creation and operation); mass production

  • •

    be compatible with the objects to be controlled

  • •

    cope with harsh enviromental conditions

  • •

    adapt to the changes in the environment and its own components

  • •

    use the available (living and non-living) material

  • •

    elaborate protocols to work with unreliable components

  • •

    be as powerful and energy efficient as possible

  • •

    apply tricks to overcome the inherently slow operation of its components

  • •

    process (encode, transmit, decode, store) information

We follow the path that we create a ”feasibility study”: suggest a solution and discuss why that solution satisfies the requirement. We deal only with the abstract level of feasibility. We have subcontractors: the scientific disciplines, to work out the details. However, we call attention to the need of refining or revisiting some of their notions and the cooperation between those subcontractors.
