§ 2.8 Finite resources

”How can the events in space and time which take place within the spatial boundary of a living organism be accounted for by physics and chemistry?” [19]

Co-existing processes may affect each other by changing resources: changing one quantity changes the other. Mathematics descibes such processes by linked differential equations, such as the Lotka-Volterra equations. A known mathematical example is the ”predator-prey” equations.
