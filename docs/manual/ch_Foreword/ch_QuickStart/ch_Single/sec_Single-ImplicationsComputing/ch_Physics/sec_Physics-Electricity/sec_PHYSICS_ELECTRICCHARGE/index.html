§ 2.3.1 Charge

Charge is the primary abstraction in connection with electrical terms. Charge generates a electric force field, and its movement generates a current (furthermore, electromagnetic waves). When those secondary entities interact with some macroscopic material, their relation to that material defines some feature, such as dielectricity or resistance. Those ternary entities manifest (i.e., are measurable) only when charge is present. Experience shows that, in the presence of electric potential, different media show different resistance against transferring charges, so we define resistance/conductance as one of the media’s macroscopic features (which is connected to microscopic features by Stokes’s Law).
