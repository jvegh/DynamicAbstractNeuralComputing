§ 3.6.2 Axon

We have a constant voltage at the end of the tube; the ”slow” current ”flowing out” from the tube increases and appears on the membrane, establishing the illusion that the conductance of the tube increases. Resistance/conductance cannot be interpreted when charge carriers flow into the resistor (and this is the case with the axon) from the environment: the ”slow” current produces a different behavior (increases the number of charge carriers n), see Eq. (2.30).
