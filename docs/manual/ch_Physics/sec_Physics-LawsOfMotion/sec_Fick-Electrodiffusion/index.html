§ 2.5.3 Fick’s Law for electrodiffusion

By expressing the speed through the Stokes-Einstein relation, see Eq.(2.40)

d⁢Vd⁢t=D*R*R*Tq*F*F*d2⁢Cd⁢x2=(T*R2q*F2)*D*d2⁢Cd⁢x2 (2.15)

Or, alternatively,

d⁢Vd⁢t=(T*RF)2⁢k6*q*π*η*a*d2⁢Cd⁢x2 (2.16)

Given that as Fick’s Second Law of Diffusion is expressed by Eq.(2.12), we can derive the ratio between the electric and thermodynamic temporal gradients, using values T=300⁢K, q=1, F= 96495⁢A*s/m⁢o⁢l, R=8.31446261815324⁢J*K-1*m⁢o⁢l-1

d⁢Vd⁢t=2.23*10-6*D*d2⁢Cd⁢x2=2.23*𝟏𝟎-𝟔⁢d⁢Cd⁢t (2.17)

The significance of that formula, that one can derive the speed of electrodiffusion in electrolytes, which are otherwise not measurable (”hopping in a breeze”: we would have to measure potential changes at distances of the size of the electrodes, with picosec resolution while the electrolytic electrodes cause nearly msec delays).
